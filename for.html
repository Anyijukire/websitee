Born a crime (Trevor Noah)

In born a crime by Trevor Noah (Novel) the author tries to potray very many concepts that are still taking place int he present world.
The paragraph below caught my attention. page 253

Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife. For a long time I wondered why he ever married a woman like my mom in the first place, as she was the opposite of that in every way. If he wanted a woman to bow to him, there were plenty of girls back in Tzaneen being raised solely for that purpose. The way my mother always explained it, the traditional man wants a woman to be subservient, but he never falls in love with subservient women. He’s attracted to independent women. “He’s like an exotic bird collector,” she said. “He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.

In this context, I realise that there's a theme of gender ineqality;
This is potrayed when Able expects Patricia to behave like the traditional women where the man is superior and the woman is inferior.
There is also a theme of independence as potrayed by Patricia. She is aware of what her abilities are, she knows she can be able to survive even when she has no husband.
I also notices that there's superiolity as Able feels he can be capable of controlling an independent woman like Patricia. I find this so irritating because it's stated clearly that there are women trained specifically for that back in his village.
Finally, I will talk about the theme of love. this is potrayed as Able and Patricia fall in love even if they know it might not end well; this being brought about by the difference in their statuses and what they both expect from each other.

Sometimes in big Hollywood movies they'll have these crazy chase scenes where somebody jumps or gets thrown from a moving car. The person hits the ground and rolls for a bit. Then they come to a stop and pop up and dust themselves off, like its no big deal. Whenever I see that I think, Thats rubbish. Getting thrown out of a moving car hurts away worse than that. (pg1)
In this paragraph Trevor Noah uses humor to talk about how fictional the Hollywood movies are so that there is no offence.
